Week 2

View Week 2 Notes

Monday

Today I continued with material preparation for fabrication work. I measured, marked, and cut mild steel square tubes according to the dimensions provided in the working drawing. After cutting, I checked each piece for accuracy and removed burrs using an angle grinder. I learned that accurate measurements are important to achieve quality fabrication work.

Tuesday

Today I assisted in fabricating a metal stand. I arranged the cut steel members on a flat surface and checked the alignment using a try square. After confirming the measurements, I tack welded the joints to hold the structure in position before the final welding was completed. This activity improved my understanding of proper fitting and alignment.

Wednesday

Today I practiced MIG welding. I learned how to prepare the machine by checking the shielding gas supply, adjusting the wire feed speed, and setting the correct voltage. I welded several joints under the guidance of my supervisor and observed how MIG welding produces clean and continuous welds with minimal slag.

Thursday

Today I carried out grinding and finishing of fabricated products. I removed weld spatter, smoothed rough welds, and prepared the surfaces for painting. I also inspected the completed work to ensure that all joints were strong and properly finished according to the required standards.

Friday

Today I participated in routine maintenance of workshop equipment. I cleaned the welding machines, inspected welding cables and electrode holders, checked the angle grinders for wear, and arranged tools in their proper storage places. I also cleaned the workshop to maintain a safe and organized working environment.

Week 3

View Week 3 Notes

Monday

Today I participated in the repair of a worn-out roller shaft. I inspected the damaged part, cleaned the surface, and prepared it for welding by removing rust and old weld deposits. I used arc welding to build up the worn section of the shaft. After welding, I ground the repaired area to achieve a smooth finish and inspected the work for quality.

Tuesday

Today I learned how to perform TIG welding on mild steel components. I prepared the workpieces, connected the shielding gas, and adjusted the welding machine according to the material thickness. Under my supervisor's guidance, I practiced maintaining a short arc length and producing clean, precise welds with minimal spatter.

Wednesday

Today I fabricated a rectangular metal frame using square hollow sections. I measured, marked, cut, and assembled the steel members according to the working drawing. The frame was tack-welded first to ensure proper alignment before completing the final welds.

Thursday

Today I carried out grinding and finishing operations on fabricated products. I removed excess weld metal, cleaned the weld joints, and prepared the surfaces for painting. I also inspected the completed frame to ensure it met the required dimensions and quality standards.

Friday

Today I participated in the maintenance of welding equipment. I cleaned the arc welding and MIG welding machines, checked welding leads, electrode holders, and gas hoses for damage, and organized tools in the workshop. I also cleaned the work area and disposed of scrap materials safely.
